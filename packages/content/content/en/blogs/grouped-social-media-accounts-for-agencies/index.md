### Stop Juggling Separate Logins for Every Client

Agencies face a unique and underappreciated challenge: managing social media for multiple clients without mixing up accounts, posting to the wrong brand, or drowning in a sea of disconnected logins. The traditional approach — switching between browser profiles, maintaining separate scheduling tools per client, or tracking everything in complex spreadsheets — is inefficient, error-prone, and does not scale.

The stakes are high. Posting client A's content to client B's social accounts is not just embarrassing — it damages trust, violates contracts, and in regulated industries, can have legal consequences. Yet most social media tools were designed for single-brand management. They treat all accounts as a flat list, leaving agencies to build their own organizational systems on top.

MagicSync's **Grouped Accounts** feature was designed specifically for this agency reality. It lets you organize social media profiles by business or client, keeping everything accessible from a single dashboard while maintaining clear, unbreakable separation between brands.

---

### How Grouped Accounts Work

Each "Business" in MagicSync acts as a container — a self-contained workspace for one client or brand. When you connect social media accounts (Facebook pages, Instagram profiles, X/Twitter accounts, LinkedIn company pages, TikTok, YouTube channels, etc.), you assign each one to its parent Business. The Business becomes the organizational boundary.

**Example Agency Structure:**

```
Your MagicSync Agency Dashboard
├── Client A: Acme Corp
│   ├── Facebook: Acme Corp Page
│   ├── Twitter: @AcmeCorp
│   ├── Instagram: @acmecorp
│   ├── LinkedIn: Acme Corp Company Page
│   └── TikTok: @acmecorp
├── Client B: Beta Inc
│   ├── Facebook: Beta Inc Page
│   ├── Twitter: @BetaInc
│   ├── Instagram: @betainc
│   └── LinkedIn: Beta Inc Company Page
├── Client C: Gamma LLC
│   ├── Instagram: @gammallc
│   ├── LinkedIn: Gamma LLC
│   └── Pinterest: Gamma LLC
└── Your Agency Brand
    ├── Twitter: @YourAgency
    ├── LinkedIn: Your Agency
    └── Instagram: @youragency
```

Each Business is isolated. Content from Acme Corp never appears in Beta Inc's queue. Team members assigned to Gamma LLC never see Acme Corp's analytics. The separation is structural, not just cosmetic.

---

### Benefits for Agencies

**1. Clear, Enforced Separation**

Every Business has its own set of connected accounts, content calendar, analytics dashboard, media library, and team permissions. There is zero risk of cross-posting between clients because the system does not allow it. Content created within one Business stays within that Business.

**2. Unified Dashboard with Instant Switching**

Switch between clients with a single click from the Business selector. View all your clients' upcoming posts, pending approvals, scheduled content, and performance metrics from one central dashboard — then drill into any client for detailed work. No logging out and logging in. No separate browser profiles. No bookmarks for different dashboards.

**3. Per-Client Team Access and Permissions**

Invite your clients to view their own analytics and approve posts without seeing other clients' data. Each client sees only their Business. You can also assign role-based permissions for your internal team: a junior social media manager might only access the two clients they handle, while the agency director sees everything.

**4. Consistent Branding per Business**

Define brand-specific settings for each Business: brand colors, font preferences, logo, default hashtags, and custom variables. Templates and media are stored per Business. When your team creates content for Acme Corp, Acme Corp's brand assets are automatically available — not Beta Inc's.

**5. Variable System for Scale**

Use system variables within each Business to personalize content automatically. Write one post template and reference `{business_name}`, `{city}`, or custom variables. The system substitutes the correct values per Business, making multi-client content production dramatically more efficient.

### Step-by-Step: Set Up Grouped Accounts

- **Step 1:** Create a new Business profile for each client in your MagicSync dashboard. Give it the client's name and upload their logo.
- **Step 2:** Connect the client's social media accounts to their respective Business. Each account is assigned during connection and cannot be accidentally moved.
- **Step 3:** Configure Business-specific settings: timezone for scheduling, brand variables, default hashtags, and approval workflow requirements.
- **Step 4:** Invite team members and assign roles per Business: admin (full control), manager (content and scheduling), contributor (creation only), or client viewer (view and approve only).
- **Step 5:** Start creating and scheduling content per Business. The dashboard separates everything automatically.

### Scaling Your Agency Operations

As your agency grows, grouped accounts become more valuable — not more complex:

- **Duplicate Successful Workflows:** Found a content strategy that works for one client? Duplicate the template, variables, and scheduling cadence to a new Business. Adapt, don't rebuild.
- **Cross-Client Reporting:** Generate aggregate reports across all clients or per-Business reports for client meetings. White-label options keep your agency brand front and center.
- **Manage Billing per Business:** Track account limits and usage per Business. Know which clients are approaching their plan limits before it becomes an issue.
- **Onboard New Clients in Minutes:** Create a new Business, connect their accounts, invite their team for approval access, and you are ready to post. No infrastructure setup. No tool migration.
